Project Idea

An app that matches asylum seekers with people willing to share their home free of charge, or at the very least free of rent. Functioning somewhat like a cross between a dating app, and AirBnB or Couch Surfer, but geared towards people who have extra space and are willing to lend it to those who need it. Both hosts and person seeking asylum would create a profile with some personal information, age, location ect, while also detailing each situation. The app would then match the person seeking asylum with a list of potential hosts.

I currently live in Berlin, a popular destination of people seeking asylum from all parts of the globe for a myriad of reasons. Obviously recently the war in Ukraine has seen millions of Ukrainian citizens fleeing the country, many with the goal of comg to or through berlin. Those without family or connections have been left to rely on the city for overnight accommodation as they move through Berlin, or having to rely on overwhelmed organisations hoping they can find an apartment to stay in for the short to medium term. This situation is not unique and not new, yet still, those seeking asylum are usually put in the precarious position of relying solely on overwhelmed and mostly grossly underfunded aid organisations to find accommodation for them. This app would help provide an avenue for autonomy and ease the pressure in searching for a safe space.

The prospective host would sign up to the app and fill out a profile. This profile would contain personal information like age, gender languages spoken, and a photo.They would then detail the space available including..
- Who else lives in your house/apartment with you
- Square metres of room and rest of house/apartment
- If an apartment, what floor the apartment is on and if there is a lift or not.
- Parking regulations in the area if no personal parking space is available
- If it is a shared space or not, and how much of the space ie kitchen, bathroom is shared
- If the host is requiring a split on the utilities
- If and what bedding/seating/furniture is available
- Whether or not the space is sufficiently heated or cooled
- Other various quirks and or physical attributes to the space being made available

The Host would then enter the details of who they are willing to accept into their space and for how long, which would include details such as
- The limits to the duration of the stay, be it a limit to the number of days/weeks/months or up until a particular date.
- How many people they are willing to host
- The various ages of people they are willing to host
- If they wish to host a family how many children or if they are willing to host children at all. (This could also work as a lower age limit).
- If they feel comfortable or not with hosting men or women or both
I feel these options could also be selected as “prefer not” option, where as others could be entered as “hard no” option by the prospective host, and limit on the amount of “prefer not” options could factored in when matching for with other users.

The person seeking an apartment would also fill out a form of personal information stating things like
- Age
- Photo
- Gender
- Languages spoken
- Who you travelling with, ie if you are travelling with family or not

They would then fill out a form detailing the requirements of the house/apartment you were looking for
- Who you are comfortable living with (much like the above questions given to the host, number of people,if you are comfortable staying with families, if you are comfortable staying with men or women ect)
- Whether or not you would need any specific requirements with a potential place to stay, i.e. wheelchair access.
- If they are willing or able to pay a share of utilities if staying in the short to medium term
- And of course desired location/s
Much like the above options for the host, these could be input as a “prefer not” option or a “hard no” option.

The person seeking asylum could then select a single or several locations and view all the potential hosts whose data/preferences represent the best matches. They could then view the hosts locations much like one would with airbnb, contact them through the app and organise a potential stay, all without blindly relying on a mediary to find a place for them.

In terms of hardware for this project, a computer is all that’s needed to create an app. Software required would be VS Code with create-react-app installed, assuming that the app would be created using React. However I am aware that the coding language Ruby is at least partly used for a lot of apps, including Airbnb, that incorporate much of the same features i am describing, but my knowledge of Ruby is next to none, and is also less commonly used, so it seems somewhat impractical to use.

Obviously for this app to be built someone with experience building apps would have to do it. I have none. I do not currently possess the skills to build it. However, I do have family who have left Ukraine recently, and have friends who have left similar situations in their home countries in order to come to Germany, who I could potentially ask for opinions and thoughts on the app from. It would obviously be imperative to have input from people who have gone through the experience.

I'm already in possession of the hardware and software to complete the project, however the Feasibility of making the app, assuming I'm doing this alone would probably be out of reach for me currently, at least at this moment, due to my lack of experience and skills. But I'm sure it wouldn't be out of this world to think I could at least make it part of the way, if I were to attempt a far more simplified “directory style” system.

The outcome of this could potentially mean that those who find themselves in a country where they have no family or connections, who are seeking asylum, have an avenue to look for temporary accommodation for themselves. And those that are willing to share the extra space in their apartment or house have an easy, quick and accessible way to do so. It also has the potential to ease the burden on aid and refugee organisations, who often end up being the sole mediaries to find accommodation.
